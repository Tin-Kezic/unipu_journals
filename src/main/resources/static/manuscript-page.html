Acoustics

Acoustics is the branch of physics that deals with the study of sound—its production, transmission, and effects. It encompasses how sound waves travel through different media (such as air, water, and solids), how they interact with surfaces and structures, and how they are perceived by the human ear. In practical terms, acoustics is essential in a wide range of fields, including architecture (designing buildings for optimal sound), audio engineering (recording and mixing sound), environmental science (managing noise pollution), and medicine (using ultrasound for diagnostics). Key concepts in acoustics include frequency, amplitude, reverberation, echo, and sound absorption.
